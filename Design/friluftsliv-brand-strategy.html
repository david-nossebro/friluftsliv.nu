01 — Brand Personality
Five words that define friluftsliv.nu
Grounded

The brand feels as solid and trustworthy as the trails it maps. No hype, no exaggeration — just honest, reliable information for every adventure.

Inviting

Nature belongs to everyone. The brand opens its arms wide — to the seasoned hiker, the weekend cyclist, and the first-time paddler alike.

Clear

Like a well-marked trail, the experience is never confusing. Information is structured, purposeful, and easy to act on — no noise, no clutter.

Calm

The brand carries the quiet confidence of a forest on a still morning. It doesn't shout; it guides. It reassures rather than overwhelms.

Purposeful

Every feature, every page, every word serves the user's need to get outside. Form follows function — beautiful because it works, not the other way around.

02 — Voice & Tone
How friluftsliv.nu speaks
Speak like this
Direct. Warm. A knowledgeable friend who has hiked every trail and slept in every cabin — and wants you to as well.
Not like this
Corporate. Formal. Over-enthusiastic marketing language that treats the user like a consumer rather than an explorer.
  • Hitta din nästa rutt — oavsett om det är 3 km eller 30.
  • Planera övernattningen. Packa ryggsäcken. Ge dig av.
  • Naturen väntar. Vi hjälper dig hitta vägen dit.

Voice principles: Use second person ("du/dig"). Prefer active verbs. Sentences should be short. Swedish before English in all UI copy. Avoid jargon — a 12-year-old and a 70-year-old should both feel welcome.

Primary: Pine + Mist. Accent: Ember (CTAs, highlights only). Sky for water/paddling contexts, Earth for elevation/terrain maps. Always high contrast — accessibility is non-negotiable.

Typography
Display / Headings — Serif (e.g. Playfair Display, Lora, or Fraunces)
Hitta din väg ut i naturen
Body / UI — Humanist Sans (e.g. DM Sans, Instrument Sans, or Nunito)
Välj bland hundratals rutter, stugor och övernattningsplatser. Filter efter aktivitet, svårighet och avstånd — och ge dig av.

Type scale: Large, confident headings paired with lightweight body text. Generous line-height (1.6–1.7). No more than two typefaces in the entire product.

04 — Key Message & Tagline Direction
What the brand says above all else

Core Brand Promise

"Sweden's outdoor life — planned in minutes, remembered forever."

A
Naturen väntar. Vi visar vägen.
Simple and functional. Positions the brand as a guide, not a gatekeeper. Works for all audiences.
B
Friluftsliv — för alla, överallt.
Echoes the brand name. Inclusivity-first. Emphasizes that the product covers the whole country and every activity type.
C
Din nästa äventyr börjar här.
Action-oriented. Puts the user in the center. Pairs well with a search bar or map as the hero UI element.
05 — Design Do's & Don'ts
What to build — and what to avoid

✓ Do

  • Use generous whitespace — let the content breathe like a forest clearing
  • Lead with the map or search as the hero — the product IS the utility
  • Make the filter/search interaction fast and tactile on mobile
  • Use rounded but not cartoonish UI components (radius: 6–10px)
  • Keep the navigation shallow — 2 taps max to a route or cabin
  • Use subtle terrain-texture backgrounds on landing areas
  • Design for thumb-first mobile use — most planning happens on phones
  • Show elevation, distance, and difficulty at a glance on route cards
  • Use icons that are simple and universally understood (hike, bike, paddle, ski)
  • Trust the photography — let it fill the space

✗ Don't

  • Don't use dark mode as default — this is a daylight, outdoors product
  • Don't use gradients that look like a sports-energy brand or app store screenshot
  • Don't crowd the map UI with excessive overlays and toolbars
  • Don't use more than 2 typefaces or more than 4 type sizes per page
  • Don't rely on color alone to communicate information (accessibility)
  • Don't animate for the sake of it — motion must serve navigation or feedback
  • Don't use drop shadows heavier than 10% opacity — keep surfaces flat-ish
  • Don't use generic hero images with lone hikers on summits — it alienates beginners
  • Don't over-gamify (badges, streaks) — this isn't a fitness tracker
  • Don't let ads or promoted content look like organic results
