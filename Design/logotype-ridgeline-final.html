01 — Identity foundation
Brand Alignment

The Ridgeline mark was selected because it directly expresses every dimension of the brand personality defined in BRAND.md. Each design decision has a rationale that traces back to a brand value.

Grounded
All three peaks share a common base line at y=48. The mark is anchored to the earth — it does not float. The visual weight is in the lower half, as it is in real landscapes.
Inviting
No sharp spikes — all peaks use softened angles (≥ 15° at the apex). The mark does not intimidate. Three layers of depth suggest that there is always more to explore, whatever your level.
Clear
Three polygons. No strokes, no gradients, no effects. The mark is legible as a 16×14px favicon. Every element serves the reading of "mountain landscape" — nothing is decorative.
Calm
The atmospheric perspective — pale behind, deep in front — mirrors the quality of light on a still morning looking toward the fjäll. The palette is entirely drawn from natural, desaturated tones.
Purposeful
The three layers use all three palette greens (Birch, Moss, Pine) — no colour is introduced for the mark alone. The identity and the design system are built from the same twelve tokens.
All activities
Mountains are the backdrop for every outdoor activity on the platform: hiking, skiing, cycling, paddling. The mark is neutral enough to own all contexts without privileging one over another.
02 — The mark
Anatomy & Construction

Three overlapping triangular polygons on a shared base. The coordinates below are canonical and must not be altered. The construction was tuned so that the front peak is asymmetric — steeper on the left face, shallower on the right — which reads as more natural and less generic than a perfect isosceles.

04 — Lockup
Primary Lockup

The horizontal lockup is the canonical form. The symbol sits to the left of the wordmark. The vertical centre of the symbol aligns with the cap-height midpoint of the wordmark — not the full type height. This alignment was tested across all sizes and produces the most optically balanced result.

Symbol only — icon contexts
Gap between symbol and wordmark = cap-height × 0.5. At 32px wordmark, this is approximately 16px. At 20px wordmark, it is approximately 10px. Never use a fixed pixel gap — always derive it from the cap-height of the set size.

11 — Rules
Never Do This
Don't stretch or squashScale proportionally using the SVG viewBox. Never apply independent X/Y transforms.
Don't recolour the layersSky, Earth, and Ember are not approved layer colours. Only the five defined variants are permitted.
Don't add strokes or outlinesThe polygons are always flat, strokeless fills. Strokes disrupt the depth logic at small sizes.
Don't add shadows or glowsNo drop-shadow, box-shadow, or filter effects on the mark. If contrast is needed, change the background, not the mark.
friluftsliv.nu
Don't use Variant A on coloured surfacesPrimary on Moss or Birch fails contrast. Use Variant B (mono) on brand-coloured backgrounds.
friluftsliv.nu
Don't substitute the typefaceDM Sans is the only approved wordmark typeface. System fonts, Georgia, Helvetica — all wrong, even if they look similar.
Don't rotate the markThe base line is always horizontal. The mountains sit on the earth — they never tilt.
friluftsliv.nu
Don't use wrong weights"friluftsliv" is always weight 500. ".nu" is always weight 300. Do not bold the wordmark.
Don't reverse the draw orderAlways: Back (Birch) → Mid (Moss) → Front (Pine). Reversing the order inverts the depth — the mark reads incorrectly.
